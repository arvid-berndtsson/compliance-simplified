SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria Reference

Purpose: Quick reference for all SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria and control objectives.

This reference provides technical details, implementation guidance, and compliance requirements for each Trust Services Criteria in the SOC 2 standard.

Trust Services Criteria Overview

SOC 2 is built around five Trust Services Criteria:

  • Security (Common Criteria) - Always required
  • Availability - System availability for operation and use
  • Processing Integrity - System processing is complete, accurate, timely, and authorized
  • Confidentiality - Information designated as confidential is protected
  • Privacy - Personal information is collected, used, retained, disclosed, and disposed of in conformity with commitments

Common Criteria (Security) - CC

CC1.0 - Control Environment

CC1.1: The entity demonstrates a commitment to integrity and ethical values.

Implementation:

  • Establish code of conduct
  • Provide ethics training
  • Address violations promptly
  • Lead by example

Evidence:

  • Code of conduct document
  • Training records
  • Violation reports
  • Management communications

CC1.2: The entity demonstrates that the board of directors oversees the entity's system of internal control over security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy.

Implementation:

  • Establish security committee
  • Regular board reporting
  • Board receives security updates
  • Board approves security policies

Evidence:

  • Board meeting minutes
  • Security reports
  • Policy approvals
  • Committee charters

CC1.3: Management demonstrates a commitment to attract, develop, and retain competent individuals in alignment with objectives.

Implementation:

  • Define job requirements
  • Provide training and development
  • Performance management
  • Retention strategies

Evidence:

  • Job descriptions
  • Training records
  • Performance reviews
  • Retention metrics

CC1.4: The entity demonstrates a commitment to the development of control activities that contribute to the mitigation of risks to the achievement of objectives to acceptable levels.

Implementation:

  • Risk-based control design
  • Control effectiveness monitoring
  • Regular control updates
  • Management oversight

Evidence:

  • Risk assessments
  • Control matrices
  • Effectiveness reports
  • Management reviews

CC2.0 - Communication and Information

CC2.1: The entity communicates information to support the functioning of internal control.

Implementation:

  • Establish communication channels
  • Regular security updates
  • Incident communication procedures
  • Policy distribution

Evidence:

  • Communication logs
  • Security bulletins
  • Incident reports
  • Policy acknowledgments

CC2.2: The entity communicates information to external parties to support the functioning of internal control.

Implementation:

  • Customer communication procedures
  • Vendor communication protocols
  • Regulatory reporting
  • Public disclosures

Evidence:

  • Communication procedures
  • Vendor agreements
  • Regulatory filings
  • Disclosure records

CC3.0 - Risk Assessment

CC3.1: The entity specifies objectives with sufficient clarity to enable the identification and assessment of risks relating to objectives.

Implementation:

  • Define clear objectives
  • Document risk criteria
  • Establish risk tolerance
  • Regular objective review

Evidence:

  • Objective documentation
  • Risk criteria
  • Tolerance statements
  • Review records

CC3.2: The entity identifies risks to the achievement of its objectives across the entity and analyzes risks as a basis for determining how the risks should be managed.

Implementation:

  • Risk identification process
  • Risk analysis procedures
  • Risk prioritization
  • Risk treatment planning

Evidence:

  • Risk registers
  • Analysis reports
  • Prioritization matrices
  • Treatment plans

CC4.0 - Monitoring Activities

CC4.1: The entity selects, develops, and performs ongoing and/or separate evaluations to ascertain whether the components of internal control are present and functioning.

Implementation:

  • Ongoing monitoring procedures
  • Separate evaluation programs
  • Control testing
  • Effectiveness assessment

Evidence:

  • Monitoring procedures
  • Evaluation reports
  • Test results
  • Assessment records

CC4.2: The entity evaluates and communicates control deficiencies in a timely manner to those parties responsible for taking corrective action, including senior management and the board of directors, as appropriate.

Implementation:

  • Deficiency identification
  • Communication procedures
  • Corrective action tracking
  • Management reporting

Evidence:

  • Deficiency reports
  • Communication logs
  • Action tracking
  • Management reports

CC5.0 - Control Activities

CC5.1: The entity selects and develops control activities that contribute to the mitigation of risks to the achievement of objectives to acceptable levels.

Implementation:

  • Control selection criteria
  • Control design procedures
  • Risk-based approach
  • Effectiveness validation

Evidence:

  • Selection criteria
  • Design documentation
  • Risk assessments
  • Validation reports

CC5.2: The entity selects and develops general control activities over technology to support the achievement of objectives.

Implementation:

  • Technology control framework
  • Control implementation
  • Monitoring procedures
  • Regular updates

Evidence:

  • Control framework
  • Implementation records
  • Monitoring logs
  • Update documentation

CC6.0 - Logical and Physical Access Controls

CC6.1: The entity implements logical access security software, infrastructure, and architectures over protected information assets to protect them from security events to meet the entity's objectives.

Implementation:

  • Access control systems
  • Security architecture
  • Protected asset identification
  • Security event monitoring

Evidence:

  • System documentation
  • Architecture diagrams
  • Asset inventories
  • Monitoring logs

CC6.2: The entity implements logical access security measures to protect against unauthorized access.

Implementation:

  • Authentication systems
  • Authorization procedures
  • Access monitoring
  • Incident response

Evidence:

  • System configurations
  • Procedure documentation
  • Monitoring reports
  • Incident logs

CC6.3: The entity restricts physical access to facilities and protected information assets to authorized personnel to meet the entity's objectives.

Implementation:

  • Physical access controls
  • Facility security
  • Asset protection
  • Personnel authorization

Evidence:

  • Access control systems
  • Security procedures
  • Asset inventories
  • Authorization records

CC7.0 - System Operations

CC7.1: The entity selects and develops detection and monitoring procedures using a variety of techniques and tools to monitor the security, availability, processing integrity, confidentiality, and privacy of the entity's systems.

Implementation:

  • Monitoring procedures
  • Detection systems
  • Tool selection
  • Regular monitoring

Evidence:

  • Procedure documentation
  • System configurations
  • Tool inventories
  • Monitoring logs

CC7.2: The entity monitors system components and their operation for anomalies that are indicative of malicious acts, natural disasters, and errors affecting the entity's ability to meet its objectives.

Implementation:

  • Anomaly detection
  • Component monitoring
  • Incident identification
  • Response procedures

Evidence:

  • Detection systems
  • Monitoring logs
  • Incident reports
  • Response records

CC8.0 - Change Management

CC8.1: The entity authorizes, designs, develops or acquires, configures, documents, tests, approves, and implements changes to infrastructure, data, software, and procedures to meet its objectives.

Implementation:

  • Change authorization
  • Development procedures
  • Testing requirements
  • Implementation controls

Evidence:

  • Authorization records
  • Development documentation
  • Test results
  • Implementation logs

CC8.2: The entity authorizes, designs, develops or acquires, configures, documents, tests, approves, and implements changes to infrastructure, data, software, and procedures to meet its objectives.

Implementation:

  • Change procedures
  • Documentation requirements
  • Testing protocols
  • Approval workflows

Evidence:

  • Procedure documentation
  • Change records
  • Test documentation
  • Approval logs

CC9.0 - Risk Mitigation

CC9.1: The entity considers the potential for fraud in assessing risks to the achievement of objectives.

Implementation:

  • Fraud risk assessment
  • Fraud prevention controls
  • Fraud detection procedures
  • Fraud response plans

Evidence:

  • Risk assessments
  • Control documentation
  • Detection procedures
  • Response plans

CC9.2: The entity implements security awareness and other training programs to support its security objectives.

Implementation:

  • Training programs
  • Awareness campaigns
  • Regular training
  • Effectiveness assessment

Evidence:

  • Training materials
  • Attendance records
  • Assessment results
  • Program evaluations

Availability Criteria - A

A1.0 - Availability

A1.1: The entity maintains, monitors, and evaluates current processing capacity and use of system components (infrastructure, data, and software) to manage capacity demand and to enable the implementation of additional capacity to help meet its objectives.

Implementation:

  • Capacity monitoring
  • Demand management
  • Capacity planning
  • Scalability procedures

Evidence:

  • Monitoring reports
  • Capacity plans
  • Demand forecasts
  • Scaling procedures

Processing Integrity Criteria - PI

PI1.0 - Processing Integrity

PI1.1: The entity implements policies and procedures to make available or deliver output completely, accurately, and timely in accordance with specifications to meet the entity's objectives.

Implementation:

  • Processing procedures
  • Quality controls
  • Output validation
  • Delivery procedures

Evidence:

  • Procedure documentation
  • Quality metrics
  • Validation reports
  • Delivery logs

Confidentiality Criteria - C

C1.0 - Confidentiality

C1.1: The entity identifies and maintains confidential information to meet the entity's objectives related to confidentiality.

Implementation:

  • Information classification
  • Confidentiality procedures
  • Access controls
  • Monitoring systems

Evidence:

  • Classification schemes
  • Procedure documentation
  • Access logs
  • Monitoring reports

Privacy Criteria - P

P1.0 - Privacy

P1.1: The entity provides notice to data subjects about its privacy practices to meet the entity's objectives related to privacy.

Implementation:

  • Privacy notices
  • Communication procedures
  • Notice updates
  • Compliance monitoring

Evidence:

  • Privacy notices
  • Communication records
  • Update logs
  • Compliance reports

Summary

This reference provides a comprehensive overview of the SOC 2 Trust Services Criteria. Each criterion includes implementation guidance and evidence requirements to help you achieve compliance.

For detailed implementation guidance, refer to the SOC 2 Walkthrough tutorial.
